# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Event organizers managing food vendor booth placements and event logistics. They're working in office environments during business hours, often juggling multiple events simultaneously. They need quick, confident decisions about vendor-event matches without second-guessing the AI's recommendations.

## Product Purpose

An AI-powered matching system that connects food vendors with appropriate event opportunities. The system analyzes vendor requirements (budget, location, timing, booth features) and intelligently matches them with available events. Success means event organizers trust the AI recommendations enough to act on them immediately, reducing time spent on manual vendor-event coordination.

## Brand Personality

Professional, efficient, trustworthy. This is a working tool for people making real business decisions. The interface should feel competent and authoritative—like a skilled assistant who knows what they're doing. Confidence without arrogance. Clarity without oversimplification.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI chatbot interfaces with purple gradients and glowing effects (ChatGPT clones, Anthropic Claude UI, generic SaaS AI wrappers)
- Dark mode "tech aesthetic" that prioritizes style over readability
- Overly playful or casual food delivery apps (DoorDash, Uber Eats consumer interfaces)
- Sterile enterprise software that feels like it was designed by committee

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over cleverness**: Event organizers need to scan, understand, and act quickly. Every design choice should reduce cognitive load, not add visual interest for its own sake.

2. **Earned trust through precision**: The AI makes consequential recommendations. The interface should feel precise, well-considered, and thoroughly tested—not experimental or trendy.

3. **Light mode as default**: Event organizers work in lit offices during business hours. Light mode isn't a fallback; it's the primary experience optimized for extended daytime use.

4. **Information density without clutter**: Show what matters, hide what doesn't. Event organizers need to see multiple options at once, but not be overwhelmed by unnecessary detail.

5. **Professional without being boring**: Efficient doesn't mean lifeless. Use typography, spacing, and subtle color to create rhythm and hierarchy that makes the tool pleasant to use for hours.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance as baseline. Prioritize keyboard navigation and screen reader support for event organizers who manage multiple tools simultaneously. Ensure color is never the only indicator of status or importance.
