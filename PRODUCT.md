# Product

## Register

product

## Users

**Primary Users:**
- **Food vendors** searching for suitable events (bazaars, festivals, markets) to participate in. They check available events on mobile during breaks, looking for opportunities that match their food type, budget, and schedule.
- **Event organizers** browsing vendor profiles to fill booth spots at their events. They work from office desktops, evaluating vendors based on food type, experience, and fit for their event.

**Context:**
- Vendors: Mobile-first usage during lunch breaks or downtime, quick decision-making, budget-conscious
- Organizers: Desktop usage in office settings, thorough evaluation, filling specific vendor needs

## Product Purpose

A two-sided marketplace platform that bridges event organizers and food vendors. The system makes it easy for vendors to discover suitable events through AI-powered natural language search, and for organizers to find qualified vendors for their events.

**Core Jobs to Be Done:**
- Vendors: Find profitable events that match their food type, budget constraints, and schedule availability
- Organizers: Discover and evaluate vendors to fill booth spots at their events
- Both: Reduce friction in the event-vendor matching process through intelligent search and filtering

**Success Metrics:**
- Vendors quickly find relevant events without manual filtering through dozens of listings
- Organizers efficiently identify suitable vendors for their event requirements
- Reduced time from search to booking decision for both parties

## Brand Personality

**Trustworthy, Reliable, Efficient**

This is a business tool, not a consumer app. The interface should feel professional and dependable, like a service that vendors and organizers can count on for their livelihood. Efficiency is paramount: users need to find what they're looking for quickly, with confidence in the results.

**Tone:**
- Professional but approachable
- Clear and direct communication
- Data-driven and factual
- Helpful without being overly casual

## Anti-references

**Explicitly avoid:**
- Common AI aesthetic patterns (purple gradients, neon glows, glassmorphism)
- Dark backgrounds and "tech startup" dark mode defaults
- Overly decorative or trendy design elements
- SaaS template aesthetics (hero metrics, gradient text, side-stripe borders)
- Consumer app playfulness (this is a business tool)

**What this is NOT:**
- A flashy consumer app
- A generic SaaS dashboard
- An AI demo showcasing visual effects
- A social network or entertainment platform

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over cleverness**: Information hierarchy should be immediately obvious. Vendors and organizers need to scan, evaluate, and decide quickly. No decorative elements that don't serve the core task.

2. **Mobile-first for vendors, desktop-optimized for organizers**: The dual-user nature means responsive design isn't just about screen size, it's about different workflows. Vendors need thumb-friendly mobile interfaces; organizers need information-dense desktop layouts.

3. **Trust through transparency**: Show real data, real photos, real details. The AI search is a tool, not a black box. Users should understand why they're seeing specific results.

4. **Efficiency as a feature**: Every interaction should feel fast and purposeful. Reduce clicks, eliminate unnecessary steps, make common tasks obvious.

5. **Professional reliability**: This platform affects people's businesses and income. The design should communicate stability and dependability, not experimentation or trendiness.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific WCAG compliance requirements at this stage. Standard web accessibility best practices apply (semantic HTML, keyboard navigation, sufficient color contrast for readability).
